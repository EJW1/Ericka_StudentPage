Adam Jonas

The screwdriver in the internet swiss army knife

Since grad school, I worked in player development for the Milwaukee Brewers and managed an amateur development academy in the Dominican Republic where we earned more than $14 million in contracts in the first 18 months of operations. In 2011, I founded Major League Draft Services, where we advised young amateur players on how to navigate the intricacies of the MLB Draft. During the process, I discovered I passionately enjoy building applications. After Avi's Skillshare class, I was hooked.

In baseball, talent scouts frequently encounter prospects that lack polish, but have a tremendous up-side due to their off-the-field characteristics. It is the scout's job to see through the athlete's flaws that can be corrected with experience and envision player's future potential. I am raw, but I want this.
